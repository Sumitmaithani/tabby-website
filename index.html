Tabby

A quiet home for your links.

Tabby is a macOS menu bar app for saving website links. No accounts, no servers, no syncing dashboards. Your bookmarks live on your machine as a single file you own.

Download for macOS v1.0 · 4.2 MB · Apple Silicon & Intel

The problem

Browser bookmarks are buried two clicks deep, scattered across three browsers, and lost the moment you switch machines. Bookmark managers solve this by selling your reading history to advertisers and locking your data behind a subscription.

Tabby does neither. It sits in your menu bar. You hit a shortcut. You save a link. That's the whole product.

How it works

Storage Local JSON file
Sync Optional, via iCloud Drive
Shortcut ⌘⇧L
Export CSV, JSON, HTML, Markdown, PDF
Requires macOS 13 or later
Price Free

Screens

menu bar dropdown

The dropdown is the whole app. Search at the top. Links below. Tags on the side. Cmd-click to edit, return to open.

Notes

The export formats matter more than they seem. Bookmarks outlive the apps that store them. If Tabby disappears tomorrow, your data opens in any text editor, imports into any browser, prints to PDF.

That's the whole point.

Install

Download the DMG. Open it. Drag Tabby into Applications. Launch it once and grant accessibility access so the keyboard shortcut works. The icon appears in your menu bar. That's setup.

To uninstall, drag Tabby to the trash. Your data lives in ~/Library/Application Support/Tabby and stays there unless you remove it yourself.
